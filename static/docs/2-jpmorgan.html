2024 marked the seventh consecutive year of record revenue for JPMorgan Chase. The firm extended credit and raised capital totaling $2.8 trillion for its clients and, on a daily basis, moves over $10 trillion in 120+ currencies and more than 160 countries, while safeguarding over $35 trillion in assets.

On the geopolitical environment
“Across the globe, 2024 was yet another year of significant challenges, from the terrible ongoing war and violence in Ukraine and conflicts in the Middle East to ongoing terrorist activity and growing geopolitical tensions, importantly with China.”

Dimon repeatedly warns that the post-WWII economic order is under more strain than at any point in his career and that “keeping our alliances together, both militarily and economically, is essential.”

On tariffs and growth (April 2025 update)

In his 2024 letter and subsequent media commentary, Dimon stated:

“Whether or not the menu of tariffs causes a recession remains in question, but it will slow down growth.”

He notes that economic fragmentation from long-standing allies “may be disastrous” for the United States, weakening both economic competitiveness and the dollar’s reserve-currency status over time.

On the role of banking

Dimon emphasises that the bank is “champions of banking’s essential role in a community — its potential for bringing people together, for enabling companies and individuals to attain their goals, and for being a source of strength in difficult times.”
