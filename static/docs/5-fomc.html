Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System · Washington, D.C.

Federal Open Market Committee — Press Statement

Issued for release at 2:00 p.m. EDT · Most recent meeting · May 2026
Statement

Recent indicators suggest that economic activity has continued to expand at a solid pace. Job gains have moderated since earlier in the year, and the unemployment rate has edged up but remains low. Inflation has eased over the past year but remains somewhat elevated. In recent months, there has been a lack of further progress toward the Committee’s 2 percent inflation objective.

The Committee seeks to achieve maximum employment and inflation at the rate of 2 percent over the longer run. The Committee judges that the risks to achieving its employment and inflation goals have moved into better balance. The economic outlook is uncertain, and the Committee remains attentive to inflation risks.

Policy decision

In support of its goals, the Committee decided to maintain the target range for the federal funds rate. In addition, the Committee will continue to reduce its holdings of Treasury securities and agency debt and agency mortgage-backed securities. The Committee is strongly committed to returning inflation to its 2 percent objective.

Forward guidance language
“In considering any adjustments to the target range for the federal funds rate, the Committee will carefully assess incoming data, the evolving outlook, and the balance of risks. The Committee does not expect it will be appropriate to reduce the target range until it has gained greater confidence that inflation is moving sustainably toward 2 percent.”
How a finance analyst should read this
  • Language has shifted from “risks moving into balance” toward a watchful stance — rates likely on hold near-term
  • Continued QT pace implies persistent duration supply to absorb in the Treasury market
  • Forward path of rates is conditional on inflation re-accelerating or labour market loosening
  • Equity-market read-through: rates-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities, regional banks) re-price most on guidance shifts
