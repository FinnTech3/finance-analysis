Net sales of $391.0 billion grew 2% over FY2023, with Services revenue reaching an all-time record. iPhone remained the largest single contributor at $201.2 billion (51% of total revenue), followed by Services at $96.2 billion (25%).

Revenue by category (FY2024)

  • Services: $96.2B — 25% of net sales (+13% YoY, all-time record)
  • Mac: $30.0B — 8% of net sales (+2% YoY)
  • iPad: $26.7B — 7% of net sales (-6% YoY)
  • Wearables, Home & Accessories: $37.0B — 9% of net sales (-7% YoY)
Item 1A — Selected risk factors

From the company’s own filing:

“The Company’s business, results of operations and financial condition could be materially adversely affected by the highly competitive markets in which it competes; rapid technological changes; supply chain concentrations and geographic risks in Asia; and the regulatory environment for digital services, including ongoing antitrust scrutiny in the U.S. and EU.”
Capital return program

During FY2024 Apple returned $110.9 billion to shareholders, comprising $94.9 billion of share repurchases and $15.2 billion of dividends. The Board authorised an additional $110 billion repurchase programme in May 2024.
