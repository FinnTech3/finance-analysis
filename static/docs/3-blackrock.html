BlackRock ended 2024 with $11.55 trillion in assets under management — a record high — following $641 billion of total net inflows over the year. Revenue grew 14% year-on-year, reflecting positive net flows, strong performance fees, and the expansion of the firm’s private-markets and technology businesses.

Larry Fink — Chairman’s Annual Letter

In his 2024 letter to investors, Larry Fink wrote about retirement, infrastructure, and the role of capital markets in financing the energy transition.

“We need to invest in infrastructure and technology that can power growth for the next 50 years. The companies and countries that figure out how to finance this transition will be at the heart of the global economy.”

The letter highlights BlackRock’s strategic acquisitions of Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP) and HPS Investment Partners, deepening the firm’s capability in private infrastructure and private credit markets respectively.

Business segment summary
  • Equity AUM: $5.6T — including iShares ETF platform, the world’s largest by AUM
  • Fixed Income AUM: $3.0T — spanning index, active, and alternative credit
  • Multi-Asset AUM: $1.0T — institutional and retail target-date/risk allocations
  • Alternatives AUM: $0.45T — substantially expanded post-GIP and HPS integrations
  • Cash Management AUM: $0.97T — institutional money market funds and ultra-short fixed income
  • Aladdin technology revenue: $1.6B — full-stack investment-management platform licensed to 1,000+ institutional clients globally
